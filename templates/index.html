What is Climate Change?

Climate change refers to long-term shifts in global temperatures and weather patterns. It is primarily caused by human activities, especially the burning of fossil fuels like coal, oil, and gas. These activities release greenhouse gases such as carbon dioxide and methane into the atmosphere, trapping heat and raising the Earth's average temperature.

The main driver of modern climate change is the increase in atmospheric carbon dioxide levels since the Industrial Revolution. As we burn more fossil fuels for electricity, heat, and transportation, we release more CO₂ into the atmosphere. Deforestation and industrial agriculture also contribute to this problem.

Why is Climate Change a Serious Global Issue?

Environmental Impacts

  • Rising Sea Levels: Melting ice sheets and glaciers threaten coastal communities and small island nations.
  • Extreme Weather: Climate change increases the frequency and severity of hurricanes, droughts, floods, and heatwaves.
  • Ecosystem Disruption: Many species face extinction due to habitat loss and changing climates.
  • Ocean Acidification: Increased CO₂ absorption by oceans harms marine life and fisheries.

Human and Economic Impacts

  • Health Risks: Increased heat-related illnesses, disease spread, and respiratory problems from poor air quality.
  • Food Security: Changing weather patterns disrupt agriculture and reduce crop yields.
  • Economic Loss: Climate disasters damage infrastructure and cost billions of dollars.
  • Climate Refugee Crisis: Environmental disasters force millions of people to migrate.

Global Concerns

The poorest and most vulnerable communities are affected most severely, even though they contribute least to the problem. Climate change is a global crisis that requires urgent, coordinated action from governments, businesses, and individuals.

What Actions Can People Take?

♻️ Reduce Waste

Recycle whenever possible, compost organic waste, and reduce single-use plastics.

🚌 Use Public Transport

Choose buses, trains, or carpools over driving alone to reduce per-person emissions.

🚴 Ride a Bike

Cycling is a zero-emission way to travel, great for your health and short trips.

🌱 Plant Trees

Trees absorb CO₂ and provide oxygen. Reforestation makes a direct impact.

💡 Save Energy

Use LED bulbs, turn off lights, and use energy-efficient appliances.

💧 Conserve Water

Take shorter showers, fix leaks, and use water-efficient fixtures.
